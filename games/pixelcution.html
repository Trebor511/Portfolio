Pixelcution

FPS Dungeon Crawler Rogue-lite • Unreal Engine 4.27 • C++

Overview

Pixelcution is a fast-paced top-down action shooter developed in Unreal Engine 4.27 by a team of five, with myself acting as the lead developer. The game features a dynamic weapon system, multiple enemy archetypes, a procedurally generated dungeon, and a roguelite-inspired relic upgrade system.

As the lead developer, I coordinated the technical direction, guided team members through feature implementation, and contributed major gameplay systems—including movement, weapon design, enemy behaviors, and level structure.

Core Gameplay Systems

  • Weapon System: Pistol, Assault Rifle, Shotgun, Grenades, RPG—each with unique fire modes, recoil, animations, and reload logic.
  • Enemy System: Unique enemy types such as the swordsman, magic caster, and grenadier.
  • Procedural Dungeon Generator: Runtime-generated maze made from modular rooms, including relic rooms and combat arenas.
  • Relic Progression: Collectible relics that enhance mobility, damage output, and survivability.
  • Shop System: A store to spend earned score to aquire weapons and relics for new runs.
  • HUD + UI: Fog-of-war minimap, health and stamina bars, reload bar, dynamic weapon/ammo display, and score system.
  • Movement System: Smooth directional movement, jumping, dashing, and overall character controller behavior.

Enemy Design & Behavior

A strong portion of the project relies on the enemy types. Having a variety of different enemy types was essential to the fun factor and success of the game. Some of these enemy types include:

  • Swordsman Enemies: Close-range attackers designed to swarm the player, forcing reaction and positioning.
  • Magic Casters: Flying enemies that target the player with volleys of magic. These enemies give verticality to targets, and absorb bullets with their projectiles, creating a new situation for the player to overcome
  • Grenadiers: A deadly combination of the Swordsman and Magic Caster, this enemy type is mostly stationary, hurling lethal bombs at the player. While these enemies deal a lot of damage, they can be dispatched easier due to their stationary behavior, and their bombs can be shot out of the air.
