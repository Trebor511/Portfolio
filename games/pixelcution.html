Pixelcution

FPS Dungeon Crawler Rogue-lite • Unreal Engine 4.27 • C++

Overview

Pixelcution is a fast-paced top-down action shooter developed in Unreal Engine 4.27 by a team of five, with myself acting as the lead developer. The game features a dynamic weapon system, multiple enemy archetypes, a procedurally generated dungeon, and a roguelite-inspired relic upgrade system.

As the lead developer, I coordinated the technical direction, guided team members through feature implementation, and contributed major gameplay systems—including movement, weapon design, enemy behaviors, and level structure.

My Contributions

As Lead Developer, I designed core systems, managed project structure, and ensured feature delivery across my team. My technical contributions included:

  • Implemented the complete player movement system (dash, directional movement, jumping).
  • Developed all weapons, including fire behavior, ammo logic, reload mechanics, and effects.
  • Built the game’s player controller and combat feel.
  • Designed tutorial stage and hub for players to explore
  • Helped create and tune enemy behaviors, particularly melee and magic variants.
  • Implemented player pickups (health, stamina, weapons, relics).
  • Collaborated closely using Perforce for version control.
